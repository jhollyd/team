Student Entrepreneur Program (StEP)

The StEP is a program of mentorship, scholarships, and workshops offered by the UMass Boston Venture Development Center to entrepreneurial students interested in careers in fast-growing companies. It is led by Dr. Shubhro Sen, VDC Executive Director & Assistant Vice Provost for Research.

Professor Sen has been a founder/CEO in 7 companies that he has led from ideation to market exits – IPO’s and acquisitions – over a 30+ year period. His second startup, Systems Re-engineering Research [later eCredit.com], based on translational academic became a Unicorn in 2000 and was acquired by the Internet Commerce Group. He also co-founded the Conscious Capitalism Institute and served as a trustee of Conscious Capitalism.

What the Program Offers:

  • Engagement with and membership of the Rising Entrepreneurs Association (REA) – the UMB student entrepreneurship society.
  • VDC access and co-working scholarships for selected students to assist in venture creation.
  • Mentorship to help students identify internship opportunities, develop the skills required, apply for positions, and succeed in the interview process. Mentorship may continue through graduation and first job.
